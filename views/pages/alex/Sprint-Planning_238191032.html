Alex Cook : Sprint Planning

Created by Alex Cook on Mar 03, 2022

You cannot perfectly plan for complex work but you can be open to change

Three questions

  1. What is the right work to focus on this Sprint, how well do we understand the work?
  2. How much can we get “Done” in a Sprint?
    1. Should at some point be intuitive
    2. Challenges
      1. Frequent change in team composition
      2. Lack of collaboration
      3. Frequent change in Sprint length
      4. Team members being split across teams or pulled away
      5. Frequent Interruptions that lead to context switching
      6. Partially done work at the end of a Sprint
  3. How much time do we have to spend improving how we work this Sprint?

To-Do

  • Establish a common set of understandings from when emergence, adaptation, and collaboration can occur 
  • Establish expectations the progress will be measured against
  • Convince a source of funding that the ROI of an initiative is worthwhile
  • Help people involved in the value delivery make better decisions

Aims

  • Enable transparency to progress
  • Set realistic expectations
  • Minimize waste while maximizing value
  • Harness change and new learnings for a competitive advantage
  • Have open and honest discussions about the unpredictability and complexity inherent in product development

Planning with a Product Mindset

Focus on valuable outcomes over quantity of output

Typical projects are measured on delivery of the full scope, on budget and on time, aka the Iron triangle, project management triangle or triple constraint. This however leaves out value. Even if we delivered on time and on budget, was the project actually worthwhile? 

The moment we start to measure ROI it is too late to determine value.

We can only plan with what we know (empirically)

  • How much do we waste with change control?
  • What is lost or delayed when we feel the burden of a heavy change?


Empiricism Reduces Uncertainty by taking action, not by planning more

Minimum Viable Product - the minimally usable version of the product

Cone of Uncertainty - the reduction in uncertainty of outcomes as an organization gathers more information

Creating Alignment

Planning is only effective when there is alignment to value delivery in the organization

We can create alignment through refinement, the product vision, goals and the product backlog

Sprint length < 1 month

A Sprint should be as short as possible and no shorter
