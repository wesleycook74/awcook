Alex Cook : Product Owner

Created by Alex Cook on Mar 03, 2022

The Product Owner acts as a funnel, gathering all the requirements for the product and any feedback received from the users, stakeholders and Scrum Team into a prioritized list of highly valuable PBIs in the Product backlog.

The Product Owner has the final say in the order of the product backlog and is responsible for ensuring that the items the Scrum Team works on are truly maximizing possible value.

There should only be one Product Owner per product regardless of the number of scrum teams working on the single product.


Product owners can

  • Allocate other people to gather requirements, gather market data, update PBIs…
  • Cancel a sprint if the Sprint Goal becomes obsolete
  • Work with the developers to reassess items in the Sprint Backlog if new information is brought to light midway through the Sprint
  • Decide on the appropriate time to release an increment
    • We can use “Done”, the review event and other means to determine if an increment should be release and which parts of the increment should be released and when

Product Owners cannot

  • Have others stand-in or replace them in Scrum Events
  • Allow others to have the final say in the order of the Product backlog, regardless if someone else physically reorders the backlog, the Product Owner will always be the one accountable for the prioritization


Tips and Training Materials

If you are a Product Owner, there are a lot of resources available to you, I would recommend going through this learning path on Scrum.org, Reading up on Product Backlog, Product Backlog Refinement, and Product Increment.


For Product Owners there are two main certifications you can get PSPO and CSPO both are considered to be the top-level certifications and there is minimal difference between the "quality" or industry consideration for the two. If price is a factor and you are not looking to attend a workshop, I would recommend PSPO but if you are looking for a workshop CSPO might be a better fit. Personally I like Scrum.org's approaches to scrum a little more than Scrum Alliance but you may find that you align more with Scrum Alliance. I would read up more on the Overview's and Missions of the two and find what fits your agile style more.

PSPO - Professional Scrum Product Owner

  • $200 USD for certification license only. Attending the workshop could cost around $500 USD.
  • Lifetime
  • Scrum.org

CSPO - Certified Scrum Product Owner

  • $500 USD. The cost varies based on the location from which you attend the workshop.
  • Continuing renewal required
  • Scrum Alliance
