Alex Cook : Delivering Done

Created by Alex Cook on Mar 03, 2022

The best practice or tool is the one that works for your product and your team. The Development Team’s shared understanding of the work needed to create a usable, releasable increment

  • Achievable
  • Appropriate
  • Minimum level of quality and completeness


DoD should ensure that the increment can be integrated into previous increments. 

When multiple teams work together they need a single “done” increment that gets integrated all together.

Benefits

  • Transparency of process
    • If we can get to done the process is working
  • Transparency of the increment
    • We understand what it truly means for an increment to be “done”
  • Transparency about where we are
    • We can better communicate progress
  • Transparency around where we are going
    • We can use size and complexity to forecast similar future work 
  • Transparency about sprint planning
    • We have a clearer understanding of what we can get to done

Creation Process

  • What do we need to assist maintenance?
  • How do we minimize tech debt?
  • How will we test the product?
  • What can we automate?
  • What defects always need to be resolved?
  • How will we meet performance and scalability requirements?
  • What standards will guide technical excellence?
  • How will we verify conformance?
  • How will we verify data quality?
  • How do we verify security?
  • How do we ensure legal and regulatory compliance?
  • What documentation is needed to release to prod?


Sprint goals to get to “Done”

  • Focus
  • Flexibility
  • Purpose
  • Measurable
  • Business Impact
  • Risk reducing
  • Team Consensus


Measuring and Analyzing Flow

Flow - the movement of value throughout the product development system

Objective data can help identify potential patterns

    • Track cycle time
      • The amount of time lapsed between when a work item starts and when it finishes
    • Use a burndown chart to track throughput
  • The number of work items finished per unit of time
  • Track WIP
    • The number of items started but not finished
  • Track work item age
    • The amount of time between when a work item started and when the current time if the item is still in-progress
  • Track blocked Product Backlog items (PBIs)
  • Pay attention to trends
