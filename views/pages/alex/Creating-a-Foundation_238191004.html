Alex Cook : Creating a Foundation

Created by Alex Cook, last modified on Mar 03, 2022

Engaged and fulfilled people will be more creative and productive. Strong, resilient, cross-functional and self-organizing teams provide the foundation for agile success.

Considerations

  • Personality
  • Emotional Intelligence
  • Intrinsic Motivation


Being Cross-Functional 

Working Agreements

  • Tasks or the expected activities and deliverables for the team
  • Process, or how the activities will be carried out
  • Norms, or the ways in which team members will interact with each other

Questions to Answer

  • What are our standards of quality?
  • How will we collaborate effectively?
  • How will we share information with the team and stakeholders?
  • What are our standards for meeting attendance, promptness and participation?
  • How will we make decisions?
  • How will we surface conflicts or disagreements?
  • How do we want it to be when we are in conflict?
  • How will the Scrum Values guide our interactions and our work?
  • How will we grow knowledge and skills across team members?
  • What does respectful behavior look like for us?
  • How will we monitor our performance and progress?
  • How will we hold ourselves accountable for our commitments?
  • What is our definition of done?

Purpose statement

  • What needs are we trying to fill?
  • How will we fulfill those needs?
  • Whose needs are we fulfilling?
  • Why are these needs important?
  • Special sauce - What makes us different?

Self-Organization

Learning and Development are an important aspect of self organization

  • We take ownership of our processes, we do not blame the process for our impediments or problems
  • We have a Sprint Goal that we create as a team.
  • We do not wait until the daily scrum to bring up challenges and impediments
  • We as a team, decide how and when to swarm on problems that jeopardize our Sprint Goal
  • We consistently and promptly update the sprint backlog to accurately reflect progress and new information
  • We determine as a team how we will improve and take ownership of implementing our commitments
  • We work together to resolve our conflicts and disagreements
  • We make timely consensus-based decisions

Shared Goals

We need a goal that is audacious, something we can stretch and strive for, this should be reflective in the Product and Sprint Goals

Clear Accountability

Every Scrum team member should have the authority to fulfill their respective roles.

Boundaries

  • Boundaries make it safe to self-organize
  • Time-boxes are examples of boundaries we set to create urgency, reduce waste and limit risk
  • A definition of done is another boundary the team sets on it’s limits for product quality


Effective Collaboration

A self-organizing, cross-functional team needs to learn how to collaborate effectively, there are five assets that can help with this:

Five Assets

  • Trust
      • Willingness to be vulnerable with one’s fellow team members
  • Go first
  • Be willing to say no
  • Assume positive intent
  • Avoid gossiping
  • Match words to actions
  • Be open and honest with each other
  • Share learnings from mistakes
  • Get to know each other as people
  • Productive Conflict
      • Use conflict to generate new ideas and explore different solutions
  • Commitment
      • Once consensus is reached, team members stay committed to the decision, even if we disagree with the decision we must trust our team members and the choices of the full team
      • Fist of Five - put up fingers to indicate whether or not the team agrees with a decision and to which degree
      • Roman Voting - Thumbs up for agree, thumbs to the side for go with the team and thumbs down for disagree
  • Accountability
      • Team members hold each other accountable for the commitments they have made both individually and as a scrum team
      • When we are willing to hold each other accountable we enable the ability to set higher standards
  • Shared Goals and outcomes
    • Bind the team together and provide team members with principles they can all align with and use to guide decision making

Team Progress

Stable Team

A stable team is one whose members don’t split their time too much with other teams and whose team composition doesn’t change too much over time

The goal should be “stable enough”. Enough so that we can continue to add value in spite of uncertainties.

Productive Team

Signs of a productive team 

  • Focus on team goals rather than individual accomplishments
  • The success or failure of the team is as a whole
  • Focus on quality improvement
  • Value of increments increases over time
  • Impediments are being removed
  • Team skills and knowledge is increasing
  • Productive Retrospectives
  • Are confident they can solve any problem
  • Committed to team success
  • Driven by result and take responsibility for results
  • Hyper-Transparent
  • Make value-driven, consensus-based decisions
  • Actively seek PRODUCTIVE conflict
  • Are willing to push themselves
  • Always looking to improve effectivity and productivity
  • Able to adapt fluidly
  • Take responsibility for process, tools and interactions
