Alex Cook : Sprint Retrospective

Created by Alex Cook on Mar 03, 2022

"What motivates us to change and the changes we select to improve upon?"

A set time to reflect and focus on what we can do to improve the over scrum process and communication practices. This meeting should take no longer than 3 hours for month long sprints and shorter for sprints shorter than that

  • Are we getting to “Done” consistently?
  • Are our customers and stakeholders happy with the value being delivered?
  • How effectively is the PO working with the developers?
  • How effectively is the Scrum Team at working with stakeholders?


Possible improvements should not only be visible in the retrospective but can be identified through feedback and impediments surfaced throughout the Sprint

  • What went well?
  • What could be improved?


“True self-reflection means being vulnerable, confronting limitations, and stretching to see other perspectives”

Tips for collaboration

  • Use silence
  • Use different group configurations
  • Get people moving and taking action
  • Shake things up
    • Try new retro methods
  • Have a flexible plan for the retro

Force Field Analysis

A basic tool for root cause analysis that can help you take action once the root cause has been identified. The technique is based on the assumption that any situation is the result of forces for and against the current state being in equilibrium. Countering the opposing forces and/or increasing the favorable forces will help induce a change by reinforcing positives and eliminating or reducing negatives.
