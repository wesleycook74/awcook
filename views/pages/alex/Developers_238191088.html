Alex Cook : Developers

Created by Alex Cook, last modified on Mar 03, 2022

Anyone who has a hand in moving a PBI from “Ready” to “Done” is considered a developer. This could include people who hold the workplace titles or roles of “Architect”, “Tester”, “QA Engineer”, “Software Engineer” or “Developer” while Scrum does not have titles for developers, it is known that the movement from traditional roles to a cross-functional team may be difficult.


For developers interested in Scrum, you can start with the Scrum.org learning path for developers. If you're really interested they also offer a certification PSD. You may find the sections on Delivering Done and Building Quality, helpful as well.

Architecture

The Architecture of a product is the responsibility of all developers and not just ones with the title “Architect” ​. We can ensure good architecture through planning some time each Sprint to discuss the architecture needed for the features planned in that Sprint and by having a set of guiding architecture principles that every Developer understands and follows when writing code.
