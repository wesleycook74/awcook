Alex Cook : Identifying and removing Impediments

Created by Alex Cook on Mar 03, 2022

Anything that blocks or slows the Scrum Team’s ability to deliver a valuable releasable product. The best way to increase Velocity is to remove impediments and reduce waste.

A development team and PO should be able to resolve some impediments on their own and others with some coaching and facilitation from the Scrum Master.

Any impediments that cannot be resolved in the above manner should be taken up by the Scrum Master

Flow can be maximized when impediments are minimized

Impediments can be visualized with a “waste snake” or a chain of the things the team does that members consider to be wasteful

Common Wasteful Activities

  • Manual Regression Testing
  • Environments not available when needed
  • Unknown dependencies found after starting work on a PBI
  • Filling out requests for other teams to perform activities and delays in responses to the requests
  • Filling out security review forms
  • Constant interruptions
  • A poorly documented aspect of the system which requires constant research or pulling another “skilled” member in to assist every time
  • Detailing too much of the “how” for each and every PBI


Tracking impediments and interruptions can be a good way to quantify the loss of productivity

Examples of Interruptions

  • Bugs, incidents, and production support questions 
  • Additional work assigned by a team’s manager Requests for time with team subject-matter experts 
  • A “drive-by” from a stakeholder wanting to chat with a developer about a new idea
  • Requests to estimate potential future work 
  • Getting kicked out of a conference room and having to move somewhere else to finish a collaboration session

Tackling impediments and Interruptions

Questions to answer first

  • What goal do we need to achieve? 
  • What is our desired outcome? 
  • What will happen if we don’t tackle this impediment now? 
  • What would we do if we could do anything we want to solve this problem? What constraints limit our options? 
  • Are they really constraints, or are we making assumptions? 
  • If an organizational policy or standard stands in our way, can we change it, suspend it, or work around it? 
  • How will we know if we are improving the situation (related to our desired outcome)? 
  • Whose knowledge, support, or influence do we need?

Steps and Tools

Stakeholder Engagement

Engage Stakeholders to help find methods or strategies for organizational impediments and interruptions

Self-Study/Continuous Learning

Individually research and learn ways in which we can grow and adapt our own skills to meet the needs of the team and organization

  • Individual “lacks” in skill or knowledge can lead to slowness and blocks that could have been resolved if the team and individual focused on learning and knowledge sharing

Pair Programming

  • Two developers working together at a single workstation
  • This can be used as a way to share knowledge and skills and can help to fill the need for learning and self-development
  • Qualitative evidence suggests that pairing results in 
    • better design
    • fewer mistakes
    • an increase in learning
    • faster problem solving
    • more accountability

Mini Training Sessions

Host short training sessions for anyone who needs it on different aspects of the product, scrum, industry or anything else that could help break down silos in knowledge sharing

Communities of Practice (CoP)

Form groups who have shared interests such as

  • Skills
  • Languages
  • Disciplines
  • Roles
  • Anything else


They can host activities like

  • Presentations
  • Coffee chats
  • Problem-solving sessions
  • Workshops
  • Social events
